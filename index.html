Sir Elton Hercules John CH CBE
"Live for each second without hesitation"

1947 - Born Reginald Kenneth Dwight

1962 - Formed the band "Bluesology"

1967 - Left the band to pursuse a solo career with Bernie Taupin

1969 - Released his debut album "Empty Sky"

1970 - Released his first top 10 hit "Your Song"

1972 - Released his first chart-topping album "Honky Chateau"

1975 - Earned his first number 1 song in US and UK with "Don"t Go Breaking My Heart"

1997 - Performed a revised version of "Candle in the Wind" at princess diana"s funeral

1998 - Knighted by Queen Elizabeth II for signifcant contributions to music and charity

2020 - Appointed as a member of the Order of the Companions of Honour by King Charles III

2023 - Will perform his last live concert of all time at the end of his "Goodbye Yellow Brick Road" world tour.
